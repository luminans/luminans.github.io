한글 (Hangul)

HUNDLISM

Hundlism condenses articulatory phonetics and design principles of Hangul into the trilateral process and reality.

PRIMITIVE LETTERS

Three geometric primitives implement primitive vowels while basic shapes implement primitive consonants.

BASIC VOWELS

The basic vowel letters outline their phonetic qualities: rising [ɔ], outgoing [ɑ], falling [u], and incoming [ɘ].

BASIC CONSONANTS

The basic consonant letters outline the tongue shapes when making their sounds, i.e., [n], [l], and [ŋ].

DERIVED VOWELS

The iotized vowel letters add a dot or a vertical line to their base letters: pre-iotation and post-iotation.

DERIVED CONSONANTS

Homorganic consonant derivatives add one or two strokes to base letters depending on their burst.

COMPOSITE VOWELS

A composite vowel forms a big vowel as its sound forms a complex vowel.

COMPOSITE CONSONANTS

All tensed consonants put their base letters side by side.

HANGUL WING

Hangul Wing compactly organizes important letters in vowel and consonant tables.

ABOUT THE INVENTOR

KING SEJONG THE GREAT

Inventor of 한글 (Hangul)

Congratulations on your mastering Hangul!

King Sejong the Great, the fourth king of Joseon-dynasty, profoundly affected Corean history with his suplerlative communication. In 1443, he invented a phonetic alphabet system originally named 훈민정음 (Hunminjeongeum) for the Corean language as well as its neighbors'. With optimally balanced geometric symbols and distinctive features of the Corean language, 훈민정음 implements the articulatory phonetics completely. The specification of 훈민정음 was aligned altogether seamlessly and converged toward the scientific moves in the future phonetics. He exercised the superlative methodology in the 15th century with the utmost ingenuity and rigor, invented the phonetic writing system to resolve the national miscommunication, and thus elevated it to the superlative one unraveling the hidden codes and missing puzzles even in the 21st century.
