Basic Vowels

Learn the fundamental vowel sounds of Hangul

BASIC VOWELS

Basic Vowels

For the basic vowels, their letters also outline phonetic qualities of their sounds, i.e., rising [ɔ], outgoing [ɑ], falling [u], and incoming [ɘ]. Their phonetic articulations are very distinct from each other by the vowel height and shape of the lips (FIG. 2 and 3). Notably, the soft palate vibrates lightly when we pronounce [ɑ] and [ɘ] with unrounded lips. The pursed lips to pronounce [ɔ] and [u] increase the air pressure in the front side of the oral tract and vibrate the hard palate heavily in a relative sense.

PICTOGRPAPHIC VOWELS

In addition to primitive vowels outlining primitive articulators, basic vowels outline their palatal movements.

BASIC DIVERGING VOWELS

ㅗ for [ɔ] indicates the hard palate moving up in the right articulatory profile and thus places a dot (ㆍ) for the active palate to the above the horizontal reference line (ㅡ). Likewise, ㅏ for [ɑ] indicates the soft palate moving forward or right and thus places a dot (ㆍ) for the active palate to the right-side of the vertical reference line (ㅣ).

BASIC CONVERGING VOWELS

ㅜ for [u] indicates the hard palate moving down in the right articulatory profile and thus places a dot (ㆍ) for the active palate to the below the horizontal reference line (ㅡ). Likewise, ㅓ for [ɘ] indicates the soft palate moving back or leftward and thus places a dot (ㆍ) for the active palate to the left-side of the vertical reference line (ㅣ).
